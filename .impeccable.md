## Design Context

### Users

Vue developers and visual designers use dither-ui Studio equally. They arrange, configure, preview, and export real dither-kit components on an infinite canvas without needing to understand implementation details before they begin.

### Brand Personality

Precise, expressive, restrained. The interface should feel crafted and technical without becoming ornamental, generic, or intimidating.

### Aesthetic Direction

Dark, monospace, pixel/dither identity with Japanese-minimal restraint. Studio takes interaction cues from Excalidraw: a canvas-first workspace, fast direct manipulation, minimal persistent chrome, contextual properties, and a searchable component library. It must remain recognizably dither-ui rather than visually cloning Excalidraw.

### Design Principles

- Keep the canvas primary; controls occupy space only when the current task needs them.
- Make insertion, selection, movement, duplication, zooming, and editing immediate and keyboard-accessible.
- Expose every public component as a functional demo; child-only pieces live inside the smallest valid composition.
- Serve beginners through clear defaults and progressive disclosure while preserving granular controls for developers.
- Preserve the dark dither identity, accessibility floor, reduced-motion support, and restrained visual hierarchy.
